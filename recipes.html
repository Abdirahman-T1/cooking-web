Bariis Iskukaris

Bariis iskukaris is made from basmati rice, and is typically topped with raisins, peas, and fried potatoes, onions and peppers, and served with roasted lamb, beef, goat, camel, or chicken.

servings and time it will take

  • Prep: 20 mins
  • Cook: 45 mins
  • Total: 65 mins
  • Servings: 6 servings

    • Ingredients list

      2 cups basmati rice

      2 tomatoes, chopped

      1 onion, chopped

      1 cinnamon stick

      5 whole cloves

      4 whole cardamom pods

      1 teaspoon cumin seeds

      3 cloves garlic, finely chopped

      4 tablespoons unsalted butter, or ghee

      1 pound lamb, cut into small cubes

      2 1/2 cups water

      Salt, to taste

    Equipment you will need

    Large Pot

    Sieve or Strainer

    Frying Pan or Skillet

    Measuring Cups and Spoons

    Knife and Chopping Board

    Wooden Spoon

    Lid for the Pot

    Aluminum Foil

    Step-by-step cooking instructions.

    STEP1:Gather the ingredients.

    STEP2:Wash the rice and soak it in water until ready for use (at least 10 minutes).

    STEP3:Fry the lamb for a few minutes until browned. Remove from the pot and set aside.

    STEP4:Fry the onions until lightly caramelized, about 15 minutes.

    STEP5:Meanwhile, grind the dry spices add to the onions, along with the garlic. Cook for a minute, until fragrant.

    STEP6:Add the diced tomatoes and cook until they break down into a thick sauce.

    STEP7:Add the lamb back to the pot.

    STEP8:Drain the rice and add to the pot with the 2 1/2 cups water. Bring the rice to a simmer, then cover the pan with aluminum foil. Ensure the pan is well sealed.

    STEP9:Simmer for about 15 minutes or until all the liquid has been absorbed.

    STEP10:Fluff with a fork, and serve.

    Somali sambusa

    Somali sambusa is a savory, deep-fried pastry filled with spiced meat, vegetables, or lentils. It features a crispy, golden-brown exterior and a flavorful, aromatic filling.

    Popular during Ramadan and special occasions, sambusa is enjoyed as a snack or appetizer, offering a delicious combination of textures and spices.

    servings and time it will take

  • Prep: 30 minutes
  • Cook: 45 minutes
  • Total: 75 minutes
  • Servings: 12 servings

    • Ingredients list

      1 pounds ground beef/lamb

      2 tablespoons oil

      1 small onion, chopped

      2 teaspoons coriander powder

      2 tablespoons cilantro, finely chopped

      green onion, chopped

      2 teaspoon cumin powder

      2 teaspoon cardamom powder

      1 teaspoon salt

      1 teaspoon pepper

      1 green chili

      1 clove garlic, minced

      ¼ cup all-purpose flour

      ¼ cup water, or as needed

      3 cups plain all-purpose flour

      2 table spoon oil

      1 cup Luke warm water

      salt

      oil for frying

    Equipment you will need

    Large Pot or Skillet

    Sieve or Strainer

    Frying Pan or Skillet

    Measuring Cups and Spoons

    Knife and Chopping Board

    Wooden Spoon

    Small Bowl

    Rolling Pin

    Tongs or Slotted Spoon

    Step-by-step cooking instructions.

    STEP 1: Gather all the ingredients.

    STEP 2: Prepare the sambusa wrappers. Mix the flour, oil, and salt together in a bowl. Gradually add water and knead the dough for 5-10 minutes until smooth and pliable. Let it rest for 15 minutes.

    STEP 3: Divide the dough into 8 equal portions, rolling each portion into a ball.

    STEP 4: Roll each ball into a flat circle (about 10-11 inches in diameter) on a floured surface. Cut each circle into four equal pieces.

    STEP 5: Heat a skillet on medium-low and place each piece of dough on it. Let it dry on both sides, ensuring you don’t overcook them. Allow to cool slightly and separate the layers.

    STEP 6: Prepare the filling. In a skillet, cook the ground meat with oil over medium heat for about 8-10 minutes until browned. Add chopped onions and green chili, and cook until tender. Add coriander, cumin, cardamom, salt, pepper, garlic, green onion, and cilantro. Stir and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes. Let the filling cool.

    STEP 7: Make the flour paste. In a small bowl, mix flour with water to create a smooth paste.

    STEP 8: Assemble the sambusa. Take one sambusa wrapper and fold it into a cone shape. Seal the edges with the flour paste. Fill the cone with the prepared filling, and seal the top to form a triangle shape. Pinch the edges to ensure it is tightly sealed.

    STEP 9: Heat oil in a frying pan. Fry the sambusas until golden brown, about 4-5 minutes per side. Remove and drain on paper towels.

    STEP 10: Serve the sambusas warm and enjoy!

    Somali Malawah

    Somali Malawah is a soft, slightly chewy flatbread with a crispy exterior, often served as a snack or breakfast. Made from flour, water, and salt, it’s typically enjoyed with honey, jam, butter, or savory dishes like stews and eggs.

    servings and time it will take

  • Prep: 10 minutes
  • Cook: 20 minutes
  • Total: 30 minutes
  • Servings: 4 servings

    • Ingredients list

      2 cup plain flour

      2 egg

      1 1/2 cups milk

      11/2 cups water

      1 teaspoon salt

      4 tablespoons melted butter

      1 teaspoon ground cardamom

      honey for serving

    Equipment you will need

    Blender

    Non-stick Pan or Griddle

    Spatula

    Measuring Cups and Spoons

    Mixing Bowl

    Brush or Paper Towel

    Plate

    Step-by-step cooking instructions.

    Step 1: In a blender, combine flour, egg, milk, water, cardamom, salt, and the melted butter, and blend until smooth.

    Step 2: Cover the mixture and set aside for 30 minutes.

    Step 3: Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and lightly brush with oil or butter.

    Step 4: Pour a small amount of batter into the pan, swirling it to thinly cover the base.

    Step 5: Cook the crepe until it is golden underneath, about 1-2 minutes.

    Step 6: Spread a little oil or butter to prevent the crepe from sticking to the pan.

    Step 7: Flip the crepe and cook the other side, repeating the process with the remaining batter.

    Step 8: Transfer the cooked crepes to a plate and serve warm with butter and honey.
